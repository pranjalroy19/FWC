\large \textbf{Eleventh International Olympiad, 1969}
\end{center}

\section* {1969/1.}

Prove that there are infinitely many natural numbers a with the following
property: the number $z = n^4 + a$ is not prime for any natura1 number n.

\section* {1969/2.}
Let $a_1, a_2, ... , a_n$ be real constants, $x$ a real variable, and
$
f(x) = \cos(a_1 + x) + \frac{1}{2}\cos(a_2 + x) + \frac{1}{4}\cos(a_3 + x) + ... + \frac{1}{2^{\,n-1}} \cos(a_n + x).
$
Given that $f(x_1) = f(x_2) = 0$, prove that
$
x_2 - x_1 = m\pi
$
for some integer $m$.

\section* {1969/3.}

For each value of $k = 1,2,3,4,5$, find necessary and sufficient conditions on the number $a > 0$ so that there exists a tetrahedron with $k$ edges of length $a$, and the remaining $6-k$ edges of length $1$.

\section* {1969/4.}

A semicircular arc $\gamma$ is drawn on $AB$ as diameter. $C$ is a point on $\gamma$ other than $A$ and $B$, and $D$ is the foot of the perpendicular from $C$ to $AB$. We consider three circles, $\gamma_1,\gamma_2,\gamma_3$, all tangent to the line $AB$. $\gamma_1$ is inscribed in $\triangle ABC$, while $\gamma_2$ and $\gamma_3$ are both tangent to $CD$ and to $\gamma_1$, one on each side of $CD$. Prove that $\gamma_1,\gamma_2,$ and $\gamma_3$ have a second tangent in common.

\section* {1969/5.}
Given $n > 4$ points in the plane such that no three are collinear. Prove that there are at least $\binom{n-3}{2}$ convex quadrilaterals whose vertices are four of the given points.

\section* {1969/6.}
Prove that for all real numbers $x_1, x_2, y_1, y_2, z_1, z_2$ with $x_1 > 0$, $x_2 > 0$, $x_1y_1 - z_1^2 > 0$, $x_2y_2 - z_2^2 > 0$, the inequality

$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{8}{(x_1 + x_2)(y_1 + y_2) - (z_1 + z_2)^2}
\le
\frac{1}{x_1y_1 - z_1^2}
+
\frac{1}{x_2y_2 - z_2^2}
\end{aligned}
$

is satisfied. Give necessary and sufficient conditions for equality.
